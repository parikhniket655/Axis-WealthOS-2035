# Axis WealthOS 2035 — Evidence Hierarchy & Source Mapping

This document maps all facts, modeled assumptions, and original frameworks presented in the **Axis WealthOS 2035** slide deck and strategic business plan. It serves as a master reference brief for team review and judge Q&A defense.

---

## Part 1: Evidence Classification

To defend the strategy against judge scrutiny, arguments are structured into three distinct tiers:
1. **Sourced Facts (SF):** Verified historical data from Axis Bank disclosures and industry reports.
2. **Modeled Assumptions (MA):** Calculations and estimates based on financial math (e.g., WACC, cost-to-serve savings, RM capacity metrics).
3. **Original Frameworks (OF):** The unique strategic concepts, designs, and architectures created by our team.

## Part 3: Team Original Intellectual Property (IP)

These proprietary concepts and frameworks were developed by our team and should be presented confidently to the judges as original IP:
* **The "WealthOS" Concept:** The paradigm of treating wealth management as a core operating system of the household rather than a sales channel.
* **The Household Wealth Twin:** The visual and technological design mapping a multi-generational family's aggregated assets, liabilities, and milestones into a live graph.
* **The 3-Person Advisory Pod Architecture:** Reorganizing front-office talent into a tag-team (Relationship, Portfolio, and Credit) to bypass the generalist RM bottleneck.
* **The AI Advisory Factory:** The concept of automated background agents handling administrative and analytics prep to compress RM meeting prep time by 95%.
* **SPARSH Advisory Control:** The governance framework embedding SEBI suitability compliance checks directly inside the RM execution loop.
* **Grounded Feasibility v2 Calculations:** Reconciling the project's financial feasibility (Base: ₹132.7 Cr NPV / 21.4% IRR; Upside: ₹224.5 Cr NPV / 26.7% IRR) utilizing the bottleneck capacity rule rather than the sum-of-caps error.
